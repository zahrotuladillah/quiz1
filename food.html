Sate Ponorogo

Sate Ponorogo is a type of satay originating from Ponorogo, East Java. The chicken meat is very tender and the seasoning is absorbed. Sate Ponorogo is made through the process of soaking the spices, after which the roasting process will be carried out. Dawet Jabung

Dawet Jabung has its own uniqueness which is also different from dawet in other areas. This beverage is from Jabung Village, Mlarak District, Ponorogo Regency. It has white color because it uses rice flour. Unlike other dawet which are green.

A bowl of Dawet Jabung contains dawet, jackfruit, and sticky rice porridge. The gravy is made from coconut milk and sugar water. The appearance is indeed very different from other dawet, the way of serving is usually one bowl of Dawet Jabung given a saucer which is placed under the bowl.

Jenang Mirah

One of the typical Ponorogo foods that can also be used as goodies is Jenang Mirah, this Jenang or Dodol is a typical Ponorogo food that is highly sought after as goodies.

The name Mirah comes from its originator named Mirah. Jenang Mirah is made from glutinous rice, coconut sugar, and non-preservatives coconut milk. The cooking process is still fairly traditional using a wood stove. Jenang Mirah can be found at the outlet on Jalan Raya Ponorogo-Trenggalek KM 7.

The taste is sweet, legit, and has a distinctive fragrant aroma that is liked by many people. Jenang can survive outdoors for one week.

Gethuk Golan

Golan itself is a village name in Ponorogo. Gethuk Golan is made from cassava that is processed and simmered, in contrast to the process of cooking fried Gethuk.

This Gethuk Golan then is served with black sticky rice, grated coconut, and liquid brown sugar. The sweet and savory taste of Gethuk Golan is very appetizing.
